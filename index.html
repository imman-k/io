About

Bridging worlds,
building strategy.

I'm from Bihać, Bosnia and Herzegovina — a city shaped by rivers, history, and resilience. Growing up in the Balkans gave me an instinct for complexity: navigating competing narratives, reading between the lines, finding clarity in ambiguity.

I've lived and studied across four countries, served as a UN COP27 delegate in Egypt in 2022, and built my education at United World College in Mostar alongside peers from 90+ countries. Today I'm at the University of Richmond pursuing a BS in Business Administration with a double concentration in Management Consulting and Analytics & Operations — GPA 3.84.

I bring global perspective and analytical rigor to every problem I work on. I'm targeting MBB and top-tier consulting firms, and I'm particularly passionate about the intersection of higher education, AI, and women's leadership.

Personal Dimension

The art of
movement.

I have a background in dance, and I taught dance in Bosnia before beginning my university journey. For me, dance was never only performance — it was how I understood precision, timing, and the relationship between structure and freedom.

Teaching brought a different kind of challenge: breaking complex movement into learnable parts, reading a room, motivating people across skill levels. These are not separate from consulting. They are the same skills.

Dance also kept me connected to Bosnian cultural roots while I traveled and changed. It's one of the few disciplines that demands your full presence — body, breath, and mind — all at once.

"Dance taught me that discipline and creativity aren't opposites. The most rigorous structure is what sets you free."

— Imman Kajtazović
